The Thirteenth

Little is known of Midara's past, shrouded in a fog as impenetrable as the Thirteenth itself, the realm from which she hails. Whatever she once was, whatever purpose she once served, has been lost to the annals of history—erased by the Flood of Darkness. When asked about her distant past, Midara remains reticent, her answers shifting like shadows, each tale as contradictory as the void itself. And for those keen enough to discern her true nature, her place within the twisted hierarchy of Voidsent remains equally elusive.

Long before the collapse of her world, Midara had seen omens written in ash and bone. Aether whispered through her veins, speaking of fates unspooled. She walked alone, not because she feared the void—but because she understood it. In the Thirteenth, death was not the end. It was merely a gate waiting to be opened.

It was here her story truly began—not as a hero, nor as a villain, but as a witness to the unraveling. The world she once knew faded, and in its place stood ruins and echoes, all whispering the same name: Moonshadow.
